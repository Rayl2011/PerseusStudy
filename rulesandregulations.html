Rules

You should follow these rules if you are a member of Perseus' Groups.

Type 1 Rules (Highest Punishment)

1.Confidentiality of Study Materials
- All notes, educational resources, and study materials provided by the Perseus study group are for the exclusive use of registered members.
- Sharing, distributing, or reproducing these materials outside the group, either physically or digitally, is strictly prohibited.
- Members must keep all group-related information and discussions confidential and not disclose them to any unauthorized parties.
- Violation of these rules may result in immediate and permanent removal from the group, with no exceptions.

Type 2 Rules (Moderate Punishment)

1. Moderation and Enforcement
- The study group's moderators have the authority to enforce these rules and guidelines.
- Moderators may issue warnings, temporarily suspend, or permanently remove members who persistently violate the rules.
- Moderators' decisions are final, and no appeals will be entertained except in exceptional circumstances.
2. Reporting Violations
- Members are encouraged to report any suspected violations of the rules or guidelines to the moderators promptly.
- Reports can be submitted through the designated channels, such as a dedicated email address or a reporting form.
- All reports will be investigated confidentially, and appropriate actions will be taken to maintain the integrity of the study group.

Type 3 Rules (Lowest Punishment)

1. Respectful Conduct
- Members are expected to maintain a respectful, constructive, and collaborative attitude towards each other.
- Personal attacks, insults, or disruptive behavior will not be tolerated.
- Members should engage in discussions and provide feedback in a thoughtful and considerate manner.
- Disagreements should be expressed civilly and without resorting to ad hominem attacks or aggressive language.
- Violation of these rules may result in a warning from the moderators.
2. Prohibited Content
- The creation, sharing, or discussion of any content that is unethical or harmful, such as:
- Explicit or offensive language, including profanity, slurs, and hate speech
- Cyber flashing or the non-consensual distribution of explicit images, videos, or other media
- Violation of these rules may result in a warning from the moderators.
3. Continuous Improvement
- The study group's leadership reserves the right to update these rules and guidelines as necessary to address emerging issues or changes in the academic landscape.
- Members will be notified of any revisions to the rules, and they will be expected to comply with the updated guidelines.
- Feedback and suggestions from members on improving the rules and enhancing the group's operations are welcome and will be considered.
